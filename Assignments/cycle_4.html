In this assignment, we used TinkerCAD to design an Arduino circuit that would need to control three LED lights separately of each other.

I decided to do something simple that would have one LED light come on as another one turned off. I made this simply in TinkerCAD before exporting the Arduino code and importing that to the Arduino program. -- Here's the link to my TinkerCAD project: https://www.tinkercad.com/things/7gnBNOQiOxs-dazzling-migelo-amur/editel?sharecode=_u_1mQ04JinCMRQEgKtiZcEjxzxWb6_viSIwlJsAzc8

I built the ciruit using my design from TinkerCAD, only this time I included the breadboard. I plugged the circuit into my computer and ran the code. At first it seemed like the second and third lights were turning on and off in the opposite of what they were supposed to, but then I realized I might have accidentally mixed up the anode and cathode wires on these two. Once I swapped them around they turned on and off in the sequence I expected.
